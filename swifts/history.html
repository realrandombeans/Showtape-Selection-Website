History

The entire history of Swift's!

Preparations

SC Entertainment was founded in March 1992, from an entrepreneur called Avery Beaumont. Beaumont had originally aspired to franchise a CEC location, however upon visiting several he noticed flaws in their management, instead aspiring to buy out a failing franchise and turn it into a new store, with many alterations which he felt would benefit the business. While waiting for a suitable store to buy, he tasked some freelance designers to draw some concepts for 3-stage retrofits. He then took those concepts to Mr Star, an artist who had previously done work for other animatronic shows. Within a few months, a new cast of characters had been fully designed and were ready to be produced, when the opportunity arose. In February 1993, a CEC in Wisconsin closed down, which Beaumont quickly bought. He tasked an unknown company to create cosmetics and props, which were retrofitted onto the 3-stage bots. Meanwhile, another unknown company created a showtape for the premiere of the show, which has been mostly lost. Segment 1 is rumoured to be in a collector's archive, while segments 2 and 3 are considered lost media.

Opening

Swift's Pizza Place opened in August 1994, and was fairly successful until May 1996, when a CEC (using a 3 stage from a closed down store) opened up nearby in direct competition, causing Swift's to lose business and eventually close, shutting it's doors on December 30th 1996. The stage decor, bots and some decor around the store were saved and put into storage. Beaumont, however, was not deterred. Determined to succeed in the family entertainment centre market, he began planning an improved location in a new area, with enough demand and no competition. Star, who had been still working for the company doing promotional art, pitched his own unique show, the Bark Street Band, using a new stage and some modified mechs. Sometime in 1997, an ex CEI employee was tasked with modifying the pianist and guitarist mechs to fit the new cast of characters, to put on a custom stage in a new restaurant that Beaumont had found, converted from a closed down grocery store.

Conversion

As the store was converted, new cosmetics were made for the BSB, however some cosmetics from SSB survived into BSB, such as Swift's blazer (reembroidered to have a B on the pocket), bow tie and top hat as well as the Woodpecker's tartan shirt. The rest of the swift cosmetics and props were kept in storage. It opened in June 1998 and performed decently well. The CEC in Wisconsin that had competed with Swift's was now struggling financially; in April 1999 it was bought out by SC Entertainment and its 3 stage was converted into a SSB, using the old backdrops and props from the first location, with most of the old cosmetics, however new masks were made and swift had to have a new blazer made, as well as many other minor cosmetic replacements.

Opening of the New Swift's

The new Swift's opened in November of 1999, throwing an extremely popular grand opening. In January of 2002, SC entertainment bought out another CEC location, retrofitting the road stage cyberamics into swift characters with a custom stage and broadening their market. The location opened in August 2002 and was the most successful Swift's location due to the location of the store. The new location initially earned a huge boost in profits, but it was fairly short-lived. Through the early 2000s, the company began to see a decline in profits and despite their best efforts, they were losing money by 2004. Bark Street was the first store to close, shutting its doors on April 2nd 2005.

Closing

The second 3 stage Swift's closed a few months later, on October 15 of that same year. The bots and props from both locations were put into storage. The cyber location was the most profitable due to the area it was in and lasted until September 1st 2006. All the assets were put up for sale at a public auction later that year. The Bark Street bots went to an anonymous private collector while the Swift cybers were sold to an unknown theme park. The 3-stage Swift bots were kept in storage due to bids not reaching the desired amount, intended to go up in a future auction. However, no such auction came; they were left abandoned in storage before being rediscovered in 2019 and bought by a private collector, who is in the process of slowly restoring them. The Swift JR bot is mysteriously missing from the lot, some think that an employee stole it as a keepsake. As for the cybers, no evidence of them has surfaced; their location is a mystery. The Bark Street bots were restored by the collecter, though haven't been shown publicly. The only evidence comes from a few leaked photos of restored characters.
